Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 June 2026 · DPDP Act 2023 compliant

This Privacy Policy describes how Cursive ("Cursive", "we", "our", "us"), operator of cursive.world and the PD Tracker browser extension, collects, uses, stores, shares and protects personal data of users of its products and services (collectively, the "Services").

This Policy is published in compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act"), the Information Technology Act, 2000 and the rules made thereunder, including the Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011.

Quick summary: We collect only what we need to provide the Services. We don't sell your data. We don't use it for advertising. We store it on secure infrastructure in supported regions. You have rights to access, correct, and delete your data — write to privacy@cursive.world.

1. Data Fiduciary & Data Processor Roles

For the purposes of the DPDP Act, Cursive acts as a Data Fiduciary in respect of personal data we collect from you (the Data Principal). Where Cursive provides Services on your behalf to your customers or to other persons (for example, lead capture flows), Cursive may act as a Data Processor, and you act as the Data Fiduciary in respect of such personal data.

2. Personal Data We Collect

2.1 Account & identity data

  • Full name
  • Email address (used as account login)
  • Mobile number (used for OTP verification and Razorpay)
  • GSTIN (only if you choose to provide it for tax invoicing)
  • Business name and registered address (only if you choose to provide them for invoicing)

2.2 Service usage data

  • Product IDs / SKUs you choose to track in PD Tracker
  • Marketplace identifiers (Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, Myntra, FirstCry) for the products you track
  • Public product data extracted by PD Tracker on your behalf (price, rating, review count, seller name — non-personal commercial data of merchants)
  • Upload history and timestamps
  • Wallet balance, row credit balance, transaction history

2.3 Service operation data

  • IP address (logged only for security and abuse prevention)
  • Browser type and version (User-Agent header)
  • Operating system
  • Approximate location derived from IP (city-level only, used for fraud prevention)
  • Time and date of access
  • Extension heartbeat signals (extension version, install timestamp, last-active timestamp)

2.4 Payment data

  • Razorpay payment IDs, order IDs and transaction status (we do NOT see or store your card number, CVV, UPI PIN, bank account password, or any sensitive payment credentials)
  • Invoice amount, GST amount, invoice number, invoice date

2.5 Marketing & analytics data

  • UTM parameters (utm_source, utm_medium, utm_campaign) when you arrive via an ad link
  • Meta Pixel and Google Analytics 4 events related to website page views, button clicks and form submissions (subject to your consent where required)
  • Lead form inputs (name, mobile, email, service interest) when you submit a "Know More" or callback request

2.6 What we do NOT collect

Cursive does NOT collect or process:

  • Your Marketplace login credentials (username/password for Amazon Seller Central, Flipkart Seller Hub, etc.) — PD Tracker reads only public product pages
  • Your bank account number, debit card number, credit card number, CVV, UPI PIN, OTPs, or any sensitive payment authentication factor
  • Your Aadhaar number, PAN, or other government identity numbers, except as voluntarily provided for GST/IEC/Trademark assistance services
  • Biometric data, health data, or sexual orientation data
  • Personal data of any minor below 18 years of age, knowingly

9. Security Measures

Cursive implements reasonable security practices and procedures consistent with the IT (RSPP) Rules, 2011 and ISO/IEC 27001 principles, including:

  • TLS 1.2+ encryption in transit for all client-server communication
  • Encryption at rest for database backups
  • Supabase Row-Level Security (RLS) policies enforcing per-customer data isolation
  • Hashed and salted password storage (bcrypt) via Supabase Auth
  • Rotating install passwords for the PD Tracker extension
  • Obfuscated extension binaries with per-customer watermarks
  • Principle of least privilege for internal access
  • Regular dependency updates and security review of edge functions
  • Razorpay-handled payment processing (PCI-DSS Level 1 compliant)

No method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially reasonable means to protect your personal data, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

10. Your Rights as a Data Principal

Under the DPDP Act, you have the following rights:

  • Right to access: You may request a summary of the personal data we hold about you and the purposes of processing.
  • Right to correction: You may request correction, updation or completion of any inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Right to erasure: You may request deletion of your personal data, subject to retention requirements imposed by law (e.g., GST records).
  • Right to grievance redressal: You may lodge a grievance with our Grievance Officer (Section 13 below).
  • Right to nominate: You may nominate another individual to exercise these rights on your behalf in the event of your death or incapacity.
  • Right to withdraw consent: You may withdraw your consent to processing at any time, with the consequence that we may no longer be able to provide certain Services.

To exercise any of these rights, write to privacy@cursive.world from the email address registered with your account. We will respond within 30 days (or such period as prescribed by the DPDP Act and its rules).
